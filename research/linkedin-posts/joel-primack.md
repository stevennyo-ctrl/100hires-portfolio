## Post 1 — Community + customer data: A 3-layer framework

**Date**: 2026-05 (approx, "1months")
**Link**: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/joel-primack_as-a-community-professional-you-wear-many-activity-7459944947106988032-MaUf?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFZQPK8BcXnoBwVbAgFxMUiN2Lddij6pz3k
**Engagement**: 18 reactions, 2 comments

As a community professional, you wear many hats. The fun in that is that you get to grow your skills with each new experience.

Recently, I've been spending a lot of time in a world of community & customer data.

To me, valuable data means 3 things:
— Data Strategy 🤓 This step is focused on understanding what data you want in the platform you're using for your community data.

— Intelligence 📊 This step allows you to focus on making sure all the data can be connected and effectively utilized.

— Activation 🏃 At the end of the day, to me, a primary outcome of a robust data strategy is being able to activate the data to drive business outcomes.

Yes, AI can help with reducing the time needed to structure the data before it's uploaded or connected to existing data in the platform you're using for your community program.

**Why I picked this**: Primack splits community data work into three operational layers (Strategy, Intelligence, Activation), which is the kind of decomposition you only get from someone who has actually owned community operations across platforms. For a B2B SaaS AI-Native role, the closing point matters as much as the framework itself: AI doesn't replace the work of figuring out what data you want, it just compresses the structuring layer. That correctly locates where AI changes community work and where it doesn't, which is rare among community thought leaders.

## Post 2 — Jobs trends: What community roles being posted right now reveal

**Date**: 2026-02 (approx, "4months")
**Link**: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/joel-primack_jobs-trends-community-edition-no-i-activity-7426348879651995648-g8rQ?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFZQPK8BcXnoBwVbAgFxMUiN2Lddij6pz3k
**Engagement**: 82 reactions, 17 comments, 3 shares

Jobs Trends: Community Edition 💻

No, I'm not looking for a role, but I read descriptions frequently to see where organizations are focusing, investing, and building. I view this as:
— Part of my role to be aware of market trends in the community space to inform my own work
— Important for my own professional knowledge for career conversations on types of work I want to focus on vs. not, compensation conversations during a performance review cycle, etc. It's just a good thing to do to be a professional today, imho.

Let's get into my POV on some things I've noticed based on specific JDs recently 🤓

1️⃣ Community and Customer Marketing & Advocacy are not just becoming close programs, but they are now becoming intentionally intertwined.
➡️ JDs that support it: Harness (Customer Marketing & Community Manager), Profound (Customer Marketing & Community Manager), Cursor (Community Lead), Vercel (Sr. Customer Marketing Manager - Startups)

2️⃣ AEO/GEO/LLM Search Citation and Community is a powerful combination to focus on in a strategic way right now.
➡️ JDs that support it: Intuit (Group Manager, Community Engagement & Generative Engine Optimization) and HubSpot (Marketer, Unowned Communities)

3️⃣ Community Managers vs. Program Owners. There's a focus, not only at large organizations, but smaller ones that have a clear program owner with extreme focus.
➡️ JDs that support it: Cursor (Campus Lead), Profound (Program Manager, Ecosystem), and Stripe (Community Enablement Lead)

4️⃣ When a company sees the value of community they INVEST in it.
➡️ JDs that support it: Lovable is hiring a leader to hire a team now, Cursor has 3 open community roles, and HubSpot has had a few open ones.

Closing Thoughts
⭐ Community is a great space to be in right now, but it's not always easy to be in it either.

⭐ Community is going through an evolution right now. AI, scale, what a community team owns and is successfully enabled to deliver, and much more creating this dynamic experience for us who work in the space.

⭐ Community is fun and a people-first profession, so enjoy it and add surprise and delight moments into your work.

**Why I picked this**: This is the strongest single signal in the entire research set, because it is downstream evidence: it reads job descriptions in 2026 to show what the community-led growth function is actually becoming at scaling B2B SaaS companies (Harness, Profound, Cursor, Vercel, Intuit, HubSpot, Stripe, Lovable). The four patterns Primack identifies (Community + Customer Marketing intertwined, AEO/GEO + Community, Program Owners with clear scope, and companies that "see the value INVEST") map exactly onto the question a small B2B SaaS like 100Hires has to answer: what does serious community investment look like at this moment, and who is doing it. For an AI-Native role specifically, the AEO/GEO/LLM Search + Community trend is on-thesis.

## Post 3 — Communicating the value of community internally

**Date**: 2025-08 (approx, "10months")
**Link**: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/joel-primack_as-a-community-pro-if-youre-able-to-communicate-activity-7354574274071859200-Omeh?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFZQPK8BcXnoBwVbAgFxMUiN2Lddij6pz3k
**Engagement**: 43 reactions, 2 komentar

As a Community pro, if you're able to communicate the value of your work in the language of your internal audience, then it'll be a major unlock for you and its role in the company.

Marketing is different than Sales which is different from Product, etc., etc.

Meet them and have specific CTAs or opportunities to engage/support based on them the programs in place for your community, too.

Match their language and goals + provide clear next steps for them = X-Company Value 🙌

**Why I picked this**: Most community-led growth content focuses on members and customers. This post focuses on the internal audience, which is the part of the job that determines whether community gets resourced and survives leadership changes. Primack's reframing (community pros need to speak Marketing language to Marketing, Sales language to Sales, Product language to Product) is operator-level wisdom that doesn't show up in CLG playbooks because it isn't sexy enough to write a thread about. For a small B2B SaaS where the growth specialist will need to defend community investment to multiple stakeholders, this is foundational.
