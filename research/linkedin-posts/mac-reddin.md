Everyones talking about the death of content and SEO thanks to AI, but they're all missing something important.

Content is far from dead if you're actually creating content people find interesting, helpful, or would be willing to pay for.

You know what my favorite litmus test for quality content is?

"Would it be worth printing this out as a physical copy?"

All our major content has been printed as physical books - the NDR Playbook, GTN Guide for Marketers, GTN Guide for Sellers.

And that's exactly what we did again with our latest campaign.

We partnered with trumpet to create the 25 in 25. Nope, it's not a bs list of 'people you should follow'.

It's 25 tips and insights from 25 people about how you can make your selling process more buyer-centric in the second half of 2025 and beyond.

It features actionable insights from great people like Dom Odoguarti, Jen Igartua, Hannah Ajikawo and so many more (well 22 more, to be exact).

Comment "25" and I'll send you a copy.

And for the first 25 to comment, I'll also send you a physical copy along with the swag box we put together for this!

**Why I picked this**: This post establishes a framework to combat AI content commoditization. Mac introduces a physical print test to validate content depth, which directly relates to building programmatic trust within a brand ecosystem. By leveraging cross-functional partnerships to distribute tactile playbooks, Mac outlines an organic customer acquisition channel that bypasses noisy traditional ad channels. It is a highly relevant playbook for early stage growth teams seeking high distribution efficiency.

## Post 3 - Scaling community networks through warm introductions over cold outreach

**Date**: ~June 2025 (1 year ago)
**Link**: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/mac-reddin_there-are-three-ways-to-market-you-can-educate-activity-7325853188278894592-DevT?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAAFZQPK8BcXnoBwVbAgFxMUiN2Lddij6pz3k
**Engagement**: 16 reactions, 4 comments

There are three ways to market. You can educate your buyers, you can make your buyers laugh, or you can make your buyers feel something.

B2B has always been reasonably good at education.

And we're slowly sorta figuring out this humor thing.

But not a lot of B2B marketing really makes you FEEL something. For all this talk about customers first, human to human, B2B marketers often seem to forget that behind every logo, every job title, and every purchase is a person.

At Commsor, if you really boil it down, we help companies make more money by helping them get more warm intros.

But in order to get more warm intros, you need a network.

And to have a network, you have to be human.

No AI prompts or templates or shortcuts are going to help you. At the end of the day, a strong network is the single most powerful asset one can have, as a business, but also as an individual.

So when Rayna van Beuzekom pitched the idea of a speed networking video, where they'd do 15 minute networking sessions with 50 people in 2 days, I said go for it.

They decided to ask each person who showed up for advice they would have shared with their younger self, and the result was nothing short of beautiful.

And no before you sales bros get snarki, it isn't just a feel-good project for the sake of it.

We've hired two freelancers because Rayna met them doing this, and we've booked tangible pipeline directly from this video in the last 24 hours.

**Why I picked this**: Mac defines the ultimate commercial metric for Community-Led Growth: converting network capital into a repeatable sales pipeline through warm introductions. He disproves the notion that community initiatives are soft brand plays by linking a concentrated speed-networking experiment directly to freelancer acquisition and closed pipeline. This provides a framework for growth operators to scale outbound operations without relying on low-converting cold outreach channels.
