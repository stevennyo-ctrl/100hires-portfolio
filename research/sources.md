# Sources: Community-Led Growth for B2B SaaS

**Topic**: How community-led growth (CLG) works for B2B SaaS companies, 
from bootstrapping to scale.

**Why this topic**: Three reasons.
1. In an AI-saturated market where landing pages, ads, and content can be 
   generated in minutes, community remains the hardest moat to copy.
2. Most CLG content is written for consumer brands or enterprise giants. 
   I wanted the operator playbook for small B2B SaaS, closer to 100Hires 
   reality.
3. The expert pool is less obvious. Most "growth marketing" guides recycle 
   the same 5 names. CLG forced me to dig into a different operator network.

**Collection method**: 
- LinkedIn posts collected manually via profile review (Phase 2)
- YouTube transcripts collected programmatically via Supadata API + Python 
  script (Phase 3, see `/scripts/fetch_transcripts.py`)
- Long-form content collected via web research (Phase 4)

**Total**: 10 experts, 30 LinkedIn posts, 10 YouTube transcripts, plus 
supplementary blogs and newsletters in `/research/other/`.

---

## How I Picked These Experts

I started with around 25 names from initial Google + LinkedIn searches. 
Cut down to 10 using three filters:

1. **Operator credibility**: Did they actually build a community, or do they 
   just write about community building?
2. **Practice what they teach**: Is their own LinkedIn or YouTube presence 
   itself a CLG case study?
3. **Tactical specificity**: Do they share numbers, playbooks, real 
   examples, or just frameworks and metaphors?

Names I dropped (and why):
- Several "Top 10 community builder" list regulars whose recent feed is 
  pure thought leadership with no tactical breakdown.
- A few authors whose only content is selling their book or course.
- Two big-name speakers whose "community" stories are all retold from 
  the same 2017 to 2019 case studies.

What I kept: operators who show their work.

## The 10 Experts

### Tier A: Active operators currently running plays

#### 1. Rosie Sherry
- **Role**: Founder of Rosieland (newsletter + community for community 
  builders). Previously founded Ministry of Testing (sold).
- **Operator credibility**: Two communities built and one sold. Rosieland 
  publishes pricing, member counts, and workflow experiments openly.
- **Where to be skeptical**: Most case studies are for solo or small-team 
  community builders, not B2B SaaS specifically.
- **Best tactical insight from collection**: Public AI workflow experiments 
  and member amplification model (e.g., spotlighting Oleksandr Romanov).
- **Sources collected**: 3 LinkedIn posts. No YouTube transcripts 
  (her content is mostly written, not video).

#### 2. Joel Primack
- **Role**: Community @ Salesforce. Host of "The Community-Led Growth Show" 
  podcast (2021 to 2023, 51 episodes).
- **Operator credibility**: Practitioner currently in role, not consultant. 
  Podcast guests included 51 community ops leaders interviewed.
- **Where to be skeptical**: Podcast went quiet after 2023. Recent LinkedIn 
  is mostly Jobs Trends content, not CLG playbooks.
- **Best tactical insight from collection**: 3-layer community data 
  framework, plus "Jobs Trends Community Edition" methodology (naming 
  specific JDs at Harness, Profound, Cursor, Vercel, Intuit, HubSpot, 
  Stripe, Lovable).
- **Sources collected**: 3 LinkedIn posts, 2 YouTube transcripts (interviews 
  with Nisha Baxi from Gong and Joe Huber from Sprout Social).

#### 3. Lloyed Lobo
- **Role**: Co-founder Boast.AI (fintech, exited majority stake), founder 
  of Traction community (100K+ subscribers).
- **Operator credibility**: Verifiable exit. Traction conference 
  consistently ranked top by Inc, Forbes, and Huffington Post. 
  Author of "From Grassroots to Greatness" (WSJ bestseller).
- **Where to be skeptical**: His "$10M ARR with zero paid marketing" claim 
  is the central pitch of his book, but his playbook required 7 years and 
  100+ events. For early-stage SaaS, this timeline matters. Also, his 
  recent LinkedIn (2024 to 2025) shifted to mental health and personal 
  topics, less CLG-tactical content.
- **Best tactical insight from collection**: "CAMPER" framework (Connection, 
  Autonomy, Mastery, Purpose, Energy, Recognition). Shareable, repeatable, 
  applicable beyond Lloyed's specific context.
- **Sources collected**: 3 LinkedIn posts (note: used 2-year-old posts since 
  recent feed is less CLG-relevant), 2 YouTube transcripts.

#### 4. Mike Rizzo
- **Role**: Founder/CEO MarketingOps.com and MO Pros community (4K+ paid 
  + 20K+ free members).
- **Operator credibility**: Rare case of community-first, product-second. 
  Started as Slack channel 2017, became business 2019. Shows pricing 
  ($250/year), member counts, and conference attendance openly.
- **Where to be skeptical**: His community works because MarketingOps is a 
  high-need underserved niche. May not replicate for crowded categories.
- **Best tactical insight from collection**: "Marketing Ops is not 
  marketing, it's a product function" thesis. Reframes CLG as building 
  infrastructure for a community, not running campaigns at one.
- **Sources collected**: 3 LinkedIn posts, 2 YouTube transcripts.

### Tier B: Active practitioner-writers

#### 5. Brian Oblinger
- **Role**: Founder Community Strategy Academy. Co-host "In Before the Lock" 
  podcast (109+ episodes with Erica Kuhl). 20+ years building community 
  programs.
- **Operator credibility**: Long track record across multiple brands. 
  Community Strategy Academy is his own productized expertise.
- **Where to be skeptical**: Heavy consultant/educator framing now. Less 
  visibility into what current community he's actively operating.
- **Best tactical insight from collection**: "8 People-Powered Pillars 
  AI Can't Replicate". Explicit anti-AI moat thinking, directly relevant 
  to 100Hires' AI-native positioning.
- **Sources collected**: 3 LinkedIn posts, 2 YouTube transcripts (note: 
  one video URL returned 404 via Supadata. Substituted with CMX Summit 
  2022 talk on community value measurement).

#### 6. Noele Flowers
- **Role**: Community Director @ Articulate. Previously Commsor, Teachable.
- **Operator credibility**: Multiple community roles at scaling companies. 
  Currently in-role, not consultant.
- **Where to be skeptical**: Recent role transition (independent to 
  Articulate) means some content reflects consultant perspective, some 
  in-house.
- **Best tactical insight from collection**: "Participate as a model member, 
  not admin" framing. Counter to most CM playbooks that emphasize 
  facilitation over participation.
- **Sources collected**: 3 LinkedIn posts. No YouTube transcripts 
  (less video presence).

#### 7. Christina Garnett
- **Role**: Customer Trust Theorist. Author of "Transforming Customer-Brand 
  Relationships". Previously Principal Marketing Manager, Offline Community 
  & Advocacy @ HubSpot.
- **Operator credibility**: HubSpot tenure gives B2B SaaS scale experience. 
  Academic grounding (sociology references: Durkheim, Goffman, Putnam, 
  Maslow) sets her apart.
- **Where to be skeptical**: Now consultant and author. Heavy academic 
  framing may not translate to early-stage SaaS execution.
- **Best tactical insight from collection**: "Friction as withdrawal" 
  framework. Treating customer trust as a bank account with deposits 
  and withdrawals. Shareable mental model.
- **Sources collected**: 3 LinkedIn posts. No YouTube transcripts 
  (her content is mostly written or spoken at conferences).

### Tier C: Foundational operator-veterans

#### 8. David Spinks
- **Role**: Co-founder CMX (acquired by Bevy 2019). Author "The Business of 
  Belonging". Sabbatical and consultant work since 2022.
- **Operator credibility**: Built CMX from scratch into industry-defining 
  community (around 25K community professionals). Wrote the book referenced 
  by almost every other expert in this list.
- **Where to be skeptical**: Post-Bevy thinking has shifted philosophical. 
  His 2026 short questions whether tech solutions are even needed. His 
  tactical playbooks are mostly from 2017 to 2021 era.
- **Best tactical insight from collection**: "Peak moments" concept (from 
  Heath brothers, applied to community) and "Do things that don't scale" 
  for community building (Paul Graham principle applied to CM).
- **Sources collected**: 3 LinkedIn posts, 2 YouTube transcripts (Business 
  of Belonging launch party + 2026 short).

#### 9. Erica Kuhl
- **Role**: EVP and GM @ Gainsight (current). Previously VP Community @ 
  Salesforce, built Trailblazer community from 0 to 3M members over 17 years.
- **Operator credibility**: Highest-credential community operator in this 
  list. Trailblazer is THE reference case study for enterprise B2B SaaS 
  community.
- **Where to be skeptical**: Salesforce playbook is enterprise-scale. 
  Direct copy-paste to early-stage SaaS won't work. Her own LinkedIn 
  pivoted to AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) recently. Interesting but 
  unproven thesis.
- **Best tactical insight from collection**: V2MOM framework (Vision, 
  Values, Methods, Obstacles, Measures) applied to community strategy 
  planning. Salesforce-origin but generalizable.
- **Sources collected**: 3 LinkedIn posts, 1 YouTube transcript 
  (Uncommon Conversations with Common Room).

#### 10. Mac Reddin
- **Role**: CEO Commsor (community-led growth platform).
- **Operator credibility**: Building tooling for community operators. Has 
  to deeply understand the practice. Commsor pivoted multiple times, 
  showing his own learning loop.
- **Where to be skeptical**: As a platform CEO, his content has product 
  marketing undertone. Distinguish between his genuine insights and Commsor 
  GTM messaging.
- **Best tactical insight from collection**: Thoughtful B2B gifting playbook 
  (LEGO, hot sauce, calendars) and "physical print test" for content quality 
  (if you wouldn't print and mail it, it's not good enough).
- **Sources collected**: 3 LinkedIn posts. No YouTube transcripts 
  (less video, more written content).

## What I Skipped and Why

**Volume of transcripts**: I capped at 10 YouTube transcripts. Could have 
fetched 30+, but the marginal insight was low. Every additional video 
mostly repeated the same 5 to 6 core ideas.

**Big-name experts not in this list**: A few obvious names (e.g., founders 
of major community platforms) were dropped because their content over the 
past 12 months is mostly company promotion, not operator-grade tactical 
content.

**One failed fetch**: One Brian Oblinger video returned 404 via Supadata API. 
Rather than substitute with low-quality alternative, I picked a stronger 
backup (CMX Summit talk) and documented the substitution honestly.
